Our Story

We've been on both sides of the table.

Inkwell was founded on a simple observation: healthcare startups with transformative technology are failing to reach the health systems that need them most — not because their products weren't ready, but because they couldn't navigate the organizational complexity and relationship dynamics of enterprise health systems.

At the same time, the solutions available to them were either unaffordable (full-time BD hires), misaligned (success-fee firms incentivized to close fast, not right), or impersonal (agencies that treated healthcare like any other vertical).

Inkwell exists to close that gap — bringing genuine relationship capital, deep sector expertise, and a transparent business model to the healthcare startups doing the hard work of improving patient care.

We believe relationships are the product. Every introduction we make is backed by earned trust, not cold outreach mechanics.

We believe alignment matters. Our retainer-only model means we succeed when you build sustainable partnerships — not when we close deals that fall apart in year two.

We believe healthcare deserves better BD. The status quo of opaque contracts and inflated success fees doesn't serve the sector or the patients ultimately affected by these partnerships.

Common Questions

Answers to what founders ask us most.

How is Inkwell different from a traditional healthcare consulting firm?

Most healthcare BD firms charge success fees of 5–15% on deals closed, on top of retainer costs. On a $500K pilot contract, that's $25–75K paid to your consultant — money that should stay in your company. Inkwell never charges success fees. You keep 100% of every deal. Our retainer model means we're incentivized to build you a sustainable pipeline, not just close one fast deal.

What stage of startup is Inkwell best suited for?

Inkwell works best with healthcare startups that have a validated product or service and are ready to begin (or accelerate) enterprise health system conversations. You don't need to be Series A or later — but you do need a clear value proposition and the operational capacity to deliver if we open doors. Pre-product startups may benefit more from our Structural Advisory engagement first.

Why a 90-day minimum commitment?

Relationship-driven BD in healthcare doesn't produce results in 30 days. Health systems move deliberately, and trust takes time to build. The 90-day minimum isn't about locking you in — it's about ensuring we have enough runway to produce meaningful results before you evaluate the engagement. After the 90-day period, you can cancel with 30 days' notice.

What does "zero AI outreach" actually mean in practice?

It means every email, LinkedIn message, phone call, and event interaction we conduct on your behalf is crafted personally by a human who understands your company and unique value proposition, and the individual we're reaching out to. No sequences. No automation. No templates spun by AI. In a market where inboxes are flooded with AI-generated content, human communication stands out immediately.

How do you measure and report on progress?

You'll receive monthly reporting covering relationship touchpoints made, pipeline status and progression, introductions facilitated, and strategic recommendations for the next period. We track early-stage relationship indicators, not just meetings booked, because that's the honest picture of where a health system deal actually is. Transparency is a core principle, including when progress is slower than expected.

Can health systems or investors also work with Inkwell?

Yes. While our primary clients are healthcare startups, health system innovation teams can engage Inkwell to help source and evaluate startup partnerships, and healthcare VCs can engage us for portfolio support — helping their companies navigate health system BD more effectively. Reach out to discuss how we might structure an engagement for your specific needs.

What types of health systems does Inkwell have relationships within?

Inkwell's network spans academic medical centers, regional health systems, clinically integrated networks (CINs), and community hospital systems across the U.S. During discovery, we'll be honest about where our network is strongest relative to your target accounts and discuss whether our fit makes sense before you commit to an engagement.

What's the difference between the Lead-Generation Plan and the Comprehensive Retainer?

The Lead-Generation Plan (12 hrs/month) is focused entirely on pipeline generation and relationship development within health systems. The Comprehensive Retainer (40 hrs/month) allows you to direct hours across any Inkwell service — including advisory, pitch deck support, and meeting facilitation. It's ideal for startups in active deal-pursuit mode who need cross-functional BD support, and carries a volume discount.
